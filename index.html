The Opportunity

Unilever just paid $1.2 billion for a packet of gummies.

In April 2026, Unilever acquired Grüns — a daily-gummy greens brand, four years old, packet-in-container format, subscription-DTC — for $1.2 billion. Unilever had spent the previous year divesting its food division to re-focus roughly two-thirds of revenue on beauty and personal care. The Grüns deal wasn't a food acquisition. It was the first premium daily-ingestible acquisition at scale on a habit metric, and it set the comp.

Two strategic signals landed at once:

(1) The format is validated. A dissolvable daily serving + single-serve packet + container-as-ritual, priced at a premium and sold on habit — that architecture is now worth $1.2B when executed against a habit metric (Grüns: 80% daily-use, 85K verified reviews).

(2) The category above categories is unclaimed. Grüns won the greens lane. Liquid I.V. won hydration. Vital Proteins won collagen. Each owns a specific outcome slot on the shelf. No one has claimed the upstream of the supplement market itself — the mitochondrial substrate layer that every outcome supplement depends on to work. The buyer who owns it sits upstream of every outcome brand.

The comp

grüns → unilever

$1.2B in 4 years. Greens + daily packet format. Habit-metric company: 80% daily-use, 85K verified reviews. Unilever paid for the behavior, not the blend.

The buyer's shift

food out. premium ingestibles in.

Unilever divested food in 2025. Beauty & personal care now targets ~2/3 of forward revenue, with premium ingestibles as the category-crossing growth engine. Strategic-acquirer capital is flowing to habit-metric ingestible brands with platform potential — regardless of which outcome shelf they live on.

The open lane

the upstream layer

$180B+ global supplement category. Every brand in it — AG1 for daily foundation, Nutrafol for hair, Ritual for women's health, Vital Proteins for collagen — asks cells to do work. None of them deliver the cellular energy that work requires. No brand has claimed the mitochondrial-substrate layer beneath the stack. The category above every category is unclaimed.

signal is the upstream layer every supplement stack is missing — and we're the first ones here.

The Market Gap

She wasn't missing ingredients. She was missing energy.

The target buyer is defined behaviorally, not demographically: someone already stacking supplements. Age span ranges from gen z students through aging-well adults — a cross-generational buyer base. Collagen in the coffee. An electrolyte stick. A beauty gummy. A greens multi. Four to five products a day, $7.50–$9.80 on the shelf. They are never sure what any of it is doing. They keep buying because they haven't found the thing that works. They keep switching because nothing feels like a category answer.

01 · her shelf

is full.

Collagen, HA, B-vitamins, electrolytes, probiotics. Raw materials everywhere. The ingredients aren't the problem — she's already invested in them.

02 · her cells

are the bottleneck.

Without the energy to use what she's giving them, her cells are running on substrate alone. She's feeding a machine that can't fully run. Every supplement she takes asks her cells to do work they don't have the ATP for.

03 · signal

is the missing input.

A clinical-dose daily chew packet that supplies the mitochondrial substrates her cells need to produce ATP and protects the machinery where that energy gets made. Two chews per packet. Three-minute ritual. A packet a day.

The strategic consequence: signal is additive to her existing stack, not a replacement. She doesn't have to drop her collagen to add us — signal supports the cells her collagen is asking to do the work. Her AG1 feeds her cells. Signal powers them. That's the cleanest possible acquisition message in a category where every new brand is pitched as "better than the one you have."

The Solution

Two chews. One packet. The architecture embodies the thesis.

signal is a single flagship SKU built on a dual-chew architecture. Every packet contains two functionally distinct filled-chews: a substrate chew that delivers the raw materials cells use to make energy, and a keystone chew that protects the mitochondrial machinery where that energy gets made. Every packet is the thesis made literal: substrates do the work; the keystone protects the machinery doing it.

Retention · The Habit Mechanism

The first supplement she actually looks forward to taking.

Foundation supplements are a retention business. The supplement category loses 60–70% of buyers by day 60 — pills endured, powders choked, gummies regressed to candy. Grüns won on 80% daily-use. That's the metric Unilever paid $1.2B for, and it's the single number we optimize for. Four things, working together, make the daily act something she repeats.

01 · the ritual

~3 minutes. four sensory beats.

Two chews, 90 seconds each. Each one opens with a cool shell crunch and releases a warm liquid-center pop at ~3 seconds. Four beats in the daily ritual — long enough to feel like its own moment, short enough to never become a chore.

02 · the three-act arc

cool crunch. warm pop. grounded clarity.

Act 1: cool allulose shell crunch. Act 2: warm liquid-center pop at ~3 sec. Act 3: 20–60 minutes post-chew, consumers report a sense of grounded clarity — a warm, settled quality distinct from caffeine. Two feelable moments in the chew, plus one sustained body-feel afterward.

03 · the architecture

the packet is the thesis.

One substrate chew + one keystone chew, visible through the clear PET back before she tears the packet. The product's architecture is readable at a glance — no explanation needed. 2.7 × 1.5 × 1 cm per chew.

04 · the unlock

no longer a chore.

Pleasure delivers consistency. Consistency delivers daily-use rate. Daily-use rate delivers the exit multiple. That inversion is the whole mechanism — not a slogan.

The milestone gate (M5–M8): 70%+ daily-use rate by month 3 of beta. If the chew isn't trending toward that number, the format or ritual is failing and gets reworked before scale spend. This is the one metric that decides whether signal becomes a category brand or a pretty SKU.

The Format

Two chews. A packet. A container.

Nothing like this exists at the clinical-dose foundation layer today. Two functionally distinct dissolvable filled-chews with liquid-center bursts, tear-open packet with clear PET back revealing both chews, 30-packet container — a foundation supplement that reads as a product, not a regimen. Format is the entry point, not the durable moat — we expect format imitation within 18-24 months and have built the architecture to compound past it (see slide 09).

The Packet

matte front. clear back.

2 chews per packet — one substrate chew (SKU-colored), one keystone chew (signal-red). 1 packet = 1 serving = 1 full daily dose. Laser-scored V-notch for clean tear. The clear PET back reveals both chews before you open it. A packet a day.

The Chew

dissolvable. 90 seconds each.

Allulose shell, progressive dissolve, liquid-center flavor burst at ~3 seconds. Two 90-second chews = a ~3-minute daily ritual with four sensory beats. No sugar, no sugar alcohols. Distributed absorption across oral mucosa and GI as the chews dissolve.

The Container

30 packets. one month.

Deep Evening primary box. Recyclable paperboard, foil-stamped wordmark. Premium unboxing, no plastic clamshell. A monthly cadence that matches how consumers think about subscriptions.

Grüns proved the daily-packet-in-container architecture is worth $1.2B. signal applies the same ritual architecture to the upstream substrate layer — with a dual-chew format that physically embodies the category position and has no analog in the foundation-supplement space.

The two-question test for every future SKU

Question 01

is it ingestible?

Every signal product is a supplement or supplement-adjacent ingestible — food, beverage, chew, stickpack. Topical skincare, diagnostics, devices, and apps are explicitly off the roadmap.

Question 02

does it sell BECAUSE of cellular energy?

If you strip out the cellular-energy angle and the product still makes sense, it fragments the brand. If the product only makes sense as a cellular-energy expression, it extends the platform. That asymmetry is the platform discipline.

What signal will never be

signal is not a hair supplement. Not a sleep supplement. Not a focus supplement. Not an immune supplement. Not a women's / men's / teen line. Specialist outcomes are the job of specialist supplements. signal's job is the upstream substrate layer beneath them. Every banned extension above would put signal in competition with specialist brands instead of upstream of them — and collapse the category position that makes the $1B trajectory credible.

01 · subscription base

~2,700 active subscribers at $75 / month subscriber price (30-pack monthly ship) = ~$203K / month. This is the load-bearing number.

02 · one-time purchases

~300 / month at $89 one-time MSRP = ~$27K / month. Non-subscription trial buyers and gift purchasers.

03 · send a signal

Subscriber tier, 1 send / month cap. If ~55% of active subs send: ~1,500 sends × $5 = ~$7.5K / month.

Sum: $203K + $27K + $7.5K = ~$237K / month → ~$200K MRR base case with conservative load factor.

Assumptions that move the target: if subscriber retention tracks closer to the Grüns benchmark (80% annual) instead of our base 70%, 12-month subscriber base is closer to 3,800 and run-rate is closer to ~$310K MRR. If the habit metric underperforms (retention in 50–60% range), subscriber base lands near 1,900 and run-rate drops to ~$150K MRR. Every assumption compounds, which is why the M5–M8 habit-metric milestone is load-bearing, not nice-to-have.
